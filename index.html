def scatter_plot(df, x, y, title= 'title', xlabel= 'xlabel', ylabel= 'ylabel', color= 'color'):
    df.plot.scatter(x= x,
                    y= y, 
                    figsize = (8, 8), 
                    color= color
                   )
    plt.title(title)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel)
    plt.ylabel(ylabel)
    plt.show()

def scatter_plot_index(df, x, y, title= 'title', xlabel= 'xlabel', ylabel= 'ylabel'):
    plt.scatter(x= df[x], 
                y= df[y].index
               )
    plt.title(title), 
    plt.xlabel(xlabel), 
    plt.ylabel(ylabel)
    plt.show()
    
def top10_diagram(column, x, y, title= 'title', xlabel= 'xlabel', ylabel= 'ylabel'):
    df.nlargest(10, column).plot(
                                    x = x, 
                                    y= y, 
                                    kind= 'barh', 
                                    title = title, 
                                    xlabel = xlabel, 
                                    ylabel = ylabel, 
                                    figsize = (10, 6),
                                    color = '#2a6099',
                                    );

Limitations¶

- The dropped rows like null or zero values may have some valuable data that may make the analysis better and strong             results. 

- There’re no data to separate (TV Shows) or (Movies) or other shows, if there’s a column that have this data, will be much helpful to get better and clear conclusions. 

- Missing (awards) data, adding that would be helpful to get a better idea to analyze the data in subjects like the show, directors or cast. 

- Missing the currency in numerus data like budget and revenue that may give a lot of misunderstanding to the data, especially to get benefit from this project in business fields. 

- The missing of the category of the show (movie), (series), etc. gave a lack of information like the episode numbers, may affect the conclusions of the project.

- Drama is the most frequent genre followed by Comedy then Thriller.

- “The Warrior's Way” is the Highest budget movie with 4.25e+08.

- Average vote of top 10 movies by vote average is 8.19.

- The correlation between rate and budget is normal and increasing positively a little. 
- The correlation between rate and revenue is slightly positive but not so strong.
